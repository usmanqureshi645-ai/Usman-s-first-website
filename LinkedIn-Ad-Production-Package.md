# UQ Consulting — 60-Second LinkedIn Brand Film
## Complete Production Package (EXPANDED FEATURE SCOPE)

**Format:** 60 seconds · 16:9 (1920×1080) primary, 1:1 and 4:5 cut-downs for LinkedIn feed
**Tone:** Apple product launch × Stripe × Linear × luxury consulting
**Logline:** *Finance professionals juggle seven different platforms. One brings them all together.*

---

## 1. CREATIVE DIRECTION (the north star)

Every frame must answer one question: *"Does this look like it cost £50,000?"*

**Three rules that protect the premium feel:**
1. **Negative space is the luxury.** Most of every frame is empty navy or light grey. Text and UI sit in pools of breathing room. Cheap ads fill the frame; premium ads leave it almost empty.
2. **One idea per shot.** Never two features on screen at once. Never more than 6 words.
3. **Movement is slow and continuous.** Nothing snaps. Everything eases. Cuts land on the music, not on the beat-drop.

**Mood words:** quiet authority, clarity, craft, trust, calm confidence, professionalism, expertise.
**Anti-mood:** salesy, busy, flashy, "AI-generated", stock-footage, Canva, list-dumping.

## 2. COLOUR PALETTE & GRADE

| Role | Colour | Hex | Use |
|------|--------|-----|-----|
| Primary base | Deep Navy | `#0A1A2F` | Backgrounds, hero frames |
| Secondary | Royal Blue | `#1E4FD8` | Accents, key UI elements |
| Accent | Gold | `#C8A96E` | Glow, highlights, cursor, charts, premium accents |
| Light base | Very Light Grey | `#F4F6F9` | "Clarity" scenes, UI backgrounds |
| Pure | White | `#FFFFFF` | Type, key product surfaces |
| Glass | White @ 8-14% opacity + blur | — | Glassmorphism panels |

**Brand note:** Navy + gold is the site's real colour scheme (see `--gold:#C8A96E` in live CSS). Updated from original cyan brief.

**Colour grade (DaVinci, see §17):**
- Lift the shadows slightly toward navy/teal (never crushed black — crushed black reads cheap).
- Highlights warm and clean (golden-hour windows), midtones neutral.
- Subtle film halation on gold highlights and lens flares.
- Light film grain (5–8%) for texture; gentle vignette (~10%).
- LUT direction: "Kodak 2383 print emulation, low contrast, navy-teal shadow, warm golden highlight."

## 3. TYPOGRAPHY

**Primary headline:** **Manrope** (the live site's font) or **Söhne** / **Neue Haas Grotesk Display** (if Manrope unavailable).
Free alternatives: **Inter Tight** (display weight), **General Sans**, **Geist** (Vercel's typeface — clean, modern, free).
**Body / VO captions:** Inter, Light/Regular.
**Numbers / data:** Tabular mono like **Geist Mono** for the chart/comparison scenes.

**Rules:** 
- Letter-spacing tight on big headlines (-2%). 
- Weight: 300–600 only, never 700+ (too shouty). 
- Animate in via opacity + 8px upward drift, 700ms ease-out. 
- Never slide in from the side.
- Max 6 words on screen at once.

## 4. MUSIC

**Direction:** Modern cinematic corporate — a single felt-piano motif, a slow swelling string/synth pad, a soft heartbeat sub-bass, one tasteful resolve at the CTA. Emotional but restrained. No drums until ~40s, and even then only a soft pulse. No EDM, no drops.

**Reference tracks (for your composer / library search):**
- Apple-style: search "minimal piano cinematic corporate inspirational" on Artlist / Musicbed / Epidemic Sound.
- Specific moods: *"Hopeful Minimal Piano"*, *"Modern Corporate Ambient Strings"*, *"Cinematic Tech Reveal"*, *"Professional Trust"*.
- Libraries (licensed, safe for LinkedIn ads): **Musicbed**, **Artlist**, **Epidemic Sound**, **Soundstripe**.

**Arc:** 
- 0–12s: sparse piano (the problem)
- 12–40s: pad enters, builds (the solution unfolds)
- 40–55s: full warm swell (the features land)
- 55–60s: resolve to a single held chord under the logo

## 5. VOICEOVER — CASTING & DELIVERY

**Voice:** male or female, mid-40s gravitas, British RP preferred, warm not cold. Think a Deloitte partner narrating, not a salesperson. Slow pace. Pauses between lines. Never upsell inflection.

**Direction note for the artist:** *"Read it like you're quietly certain, talking to one respected peer across a desk — not to a room. You're not selling; you're explaining something that solves a real problem."*

**Emotional objective:** Recognition. Tension. Relatability. "That's my daily life."

## 9. MOTION GRAPHICS & ANIMATION PRINCIPLES

### Core Rules
- **Subtlety over flashiness.** No spinning logos. No flying text. No 3D flips.
- **Purposeful movement.** Every animation answers: "Why is this moving?"
- **Easing:** Smooth cubic-bezier (ease-in-out), never linear, never bounce.
- **Depth:** Layering (background, mid, foreground) creates 3D without overdoing.
- **Timing:** Most animations 300–600ms. Entrance 400ms, exit 300ms. Staggered animations 100ms apart.

### Glassmorphism Effects (Used Throughout)
- Semi-transparent white/gold panels (8–14% opacity).
- Backdrop blur (10–20px radius in CSS, or simulated in video with soft focus).
- Subtle border glow (gold, 1–2px, soft).
- Reflection sweeps (moving light source across glossy surfaces, subtle).

### Parallax (Depth Storytelling)
- Background UI moves slower than foreground.
- Creates false 3D without actual 3D rendering.
- Used in: Meeting Room (Scene 3), Dashboard (Scene 8), Multi-Device (Scene 13).

### Avatar/Icon Animations
- Fade-in with stagger (100ms apart).
- Glow on activation (brief, 200ms ease-in).
- Never bounce or spin (too playful for professional tone).

### Text Animations
- Opacity fade-in + 8px upward drift (600–700ms ease-out).
- Never slide from sides (feels dated).
- Type-out effect only for chat messages (natural, conversational).

### UI Element Animations
- Cards: fade-in + subtle scale (95%→100%, 400ms ease-out).
- Buttons: glow on hover (gold halo, brief pulse).
- Dropdowns: smooth ease-out expansion (not snappy).
- Progress bars: smooth linear fill (no stalling).
- Checkmarks: brief scale (0→1, 200ms ease-in) + glow.

## 10. CINEMATOGRAPHY CHECKLIST

### Camera Movement (Use Sparingly)
- ✅ Slow push-ins (0.5–2s total, on hero elements).
- ✅ Parallax scrolling (foreground slower, background faster, or vice versa).
- ✅ Macro-style zooms (3–5%, creates intimacy).
- ✅ Slow pans (left-to-right over multi-device scene, 2–3s).
- ✅ Pull-backs (wide shot after close-up, creates context).
- ❌ Whip pans (too fast, cheap feel).
- ❌ Spinning cameras (distracting).

### Lighting Must-Haves
- ✅ Warm golden-hour light on office scenes.
- ✅ Soft shadows (directional, not flat).

## 11. COLOUR GRADING & POST-PRODUCTION

**DaVinci Resolve Workflow (Grade Node Tree):**

1. **Input Node (Media):** Bring in raw footage.
2. **Curves Node:**
   - Shadows: lift slightly toward navy (RGB curve, bottom-left lifted to 20% grey, not black).
   - Midtones: neutral (straight diagonal).
   - Highlights: warm (slightly pulled toward yellow/gold in RGB).
   - Overall S-curve for contrast (subtle, not dramatic).

3. **Colour Wheels:**
   - Shadows: slight navy/cyan tint (add blue -10%).
   - Midtones: slight warm shift (add red +5%).

5. **Film Grain:** Add light grain (3–5% noise, ISO equivalent 100–200).

6. **Vignette:** Apply subtle darkening on edges (10% opacity, soft falloff).

7. **Saturation:** Reduce overall saturation slightly (-5% to -10%), but keep navy + gold saturated (selective desaturation).

8. **Output Node:** Deliver to H.264 or ProRes depending on platform.

## 12. LIGHTING REFERENCE SETUP

For on-location or 3D/CGI scenes:

     - Position Y: -8px offset, ease to 0px (upward drift effect).
   - Duration per text: 2–3 seconds.
5. **After Effects Integration (Optional):**
   - Create advanced animations (glow effects, parallax, macro shots) in AE.
   - Pre-compose, export as ProRes, import back into Premiere.
   - Link dynamic links for non-destructive re-editing.
6. **Lumetri Color (Grading):**
   - Create adjustment layer on top of all clips.
   - Basic Correction tab:
     - Exposure: 0 (neutral).
     - Contrast: +5 (subtle punch).
     - Highlights: slightly warm (+10% warmth slider).
     - Shadows: slightly lifted, navy tint.
   - Creative tab:
     - Apply "Kodak 2383" LUT if available (via LUT import).
     - Saturation: -5 to -10%.
     - Vibrance: 0 (to avoid oversaturation).
   - Curves tab:
     - Lift shadows (black point to 10% grey).
     - S-curve for contrast.
7. **Audio (Essential Sound Panel):**
   - Identify audio tracks: VO, Music, SFX.
   - **Voiceover:** 
     - Loudness: -18dB peak.
     - Apply light compression (Adaptive Limiter, 4:1).
     - EQ: Cut 100Hz and below, slight 3kHz boost.
     - Reverb: +0.5s room (Hall preset, -30dB send).
   - **Music:** 
     - Loudness: -14dB peak.
     - Pan: Center.
     - Keyframe volume for dynamic swells (lower at 0:00–0:12, raise at 0:28–0:40).
   - **SFX:** 
     - Individual tracks, -20dB to -26dB.
     - Position in stereo (subtle, mostly center).
     - Time-align with UI animations (notification sound lands on glow, etc.).
   - **Master loudness:** Normalize to -16 LUFS using Loudness Radar.
8. **Media Encoder (Delivery):**
   - Create export preset:
     - Codec: H.264.
     - Resolution: 1920×1080 (primary) or 3840×2160 (4K).
     - Frame rate: 24fps.
     - Bitrate: 12 Mbps (1080p), 25 Mbps (4K).
     - Audio: 48kHz, 320 kbps stereo AAC.
   - Queue export, render to MP4.

## 23. 4:5 & 1:1 REFRAMING TABLE

If creating vertical/square variants from 16:9 source:

| Aspect Ratio | Dimensions | Crop Type | Safe Zone | Best For |
|--------------|------------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| 16:9 (Hero) | 1920×1080 | Original | Full frame | LinkedIn feed, YouTube |
| 4:5 (Portrait) | 1080×1350 | Center crop + pan | Top 85%, bottom 85%, sides drop | Instagram Stories, TikTok, LinkedIn mobile |
| 1:1 (Square) | 1080×1080 | Center crop, equal sides | Center 75% | LinkedIn carousel, Twitter, Facebook, Instagram feed |

**How to reframe:**
1. In your NLE (Premiere, Resolve, CapCut), create new sequence at target aspect ratio.
2. Nest/import the 16:9 edit into 4:5 sequence.
3. Scale 16:9 up to fill 4:5 (will crop top/bottom).
4. Or: pan horizontally across the 16:9 source to keep key UI elements centered (dynamic reframing).
5. Render separate MP4 files for each ratio.

**Pro tip:** Shoot/edit key scenes with knowledge that they'll be cropped (safe zones). Keep text/UI elements in center 50% of frame, not edges.
